Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 2026

1. Overview

FedMuster is an internal workplace safety application used exclusively for fire drill coordination within the Federal Reserve Bank of Boston. This Privacy Policy describes how we collect, use, and protect employee information.

2. Information We Collect

  • Employee full name
  • Job role / department
  • Mobile phone number (for SMS drill notifications only)
  • Drill participation status (Safe, Evacuating, Not at Work, No Response)
  • Optional status notes provided during drills

3. How We Use Your Information

  • To send SMS notifications when a fire drill begins
  • To send an all-clear SMS when a fire drill ends
  • To display real-time evacuation status on the coordinator dashboard
  • To maintain drill history records for safety compliance purposes

4. SMS Messaging

By participating in FedMuster, employees consent to receive SMS messages from the system. Message frequency is typically 2 messages per drill event (one drill alert and one all-clear notification). Standard message and data rates may apply depending on your mobile carrier. You may opt out at any time by replying STOP to any message. To re-subscribe, reply START.

5. Data Storage

Employee data is stored securely in a cloud database accessible only to authorized administrators within the Federal Reserve Bank of Boston Internal Audit Department. Data is not shared with any third parties, sold, or used for marketing purposes of any kind.

6. Data Retention

Drill history is retained for up to 10 drill events for compliance and safety reporting purposes. Employee contact information is maintained as long as the employee is an active participant in the fire drill program.

7. Your Rights

  • You may request removal of your information by contacting your department administrator
  • You may opt out of SMS notifications at any time by replying STOP
  • You may request a copy of the data stored about you

8. Contact

For privacy concerns or data removal requests, contact the Federal Reserve Bank of Boston Internal Audit Department administrator.

Terms and Conditions

Last updated: April 2026

1. Purpose

FedMuster is an internal emergency notification system used exclusively for workplace fire drill coordination at the Federal Reserve Bank of Boston. It is not a public-facing service.

2. Authorized Use

FedMuster is authorized for use by employees of the Federal Reserve Bank of Boston who have been enrolled by their department administrator. Unauthorized access is prohibited.

3. SMS Consent

Employees enrolled in FedMuster have provided explicit consent — verbally during workplace safety briefings and/or in writing — to receive SMS notifications during fire drills. Enrollment is managed by the Internal Audit Department administrator who maintains a signed consent list.

4. Message Frequency & Content

  • Messages are sent only during fire drill events
  • Typical frequency: 2 messages per drill (alert + all-clear)
  • Messages include a direct link to report evacuation status
  • No marketing, promotional, or unrelated messages are ever sent

5. Opt-Out

Participants may opt out of SMS notifications at any time by replying STOP to any FedMuster message. For help, reply HELP. To re-enroll, contact your department administrator.

6. Disclaimer

FedMuster is a supplementary safety coordination tool. It does not replace official emergency procedures, building evacuation protocols, or the authority of safety wardens and emergency personnel.

7. Data Protection

All employee data collected through FedMuster is used solely for fire drill coordination purposes and is never shared with third parties or used for any commercial purpose.

8. Changes

These terms may be updated periodically. Continued participation in the FedMuster program constitutes acceptance of any updated terms.
